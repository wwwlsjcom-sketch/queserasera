# Exhibit Library

This library defines exactly 24 reusable exhibit modules for version 2.0.0. Use them as patterns for HTML/CSS/SVG implementation. Each module needs a conclusion-led title, caption, source/assumption note, and nearby implication text.

## 1. market-trend-index

Use for market size, adoption, usage, revenue, pricing, cost, or KPI trajectories. Prefer line, indexed line, area, or slope chart. Label whether values are measured, estimated, directional, or illustrative.

## 2. ranking-bar

Use for segment ranking, competitor ranking, feature importance, driver contribution, or benchmark comparison. Prefer horizontal bars with clear labels and deltas.

## 3. stacked-share

Use for mix, market share, portfolio composition, revenue split, budget allocation, or part-to-whole analysis. Prefer stacked bars over pie charts unless categories are very few.

## 4. grouped-bar

Use for comparing categories across periods, regions, segments, products, or scenarios. Keep group labels short and use a legend.

## 5. small-multiples

Use for repeated trend or comparison across segments, markets, cohorts, or products. Useful for data-heavy reports where one aggregate chart hides pattern differences.

## 6. value-chain

Use for industry structure, profit pools, bottlenecks, data/control points, or margin pressure. Show stages, ownership, and pressure points.

## 7. ecosystem-map

Use for platform ecosystems, stakeholder relationships, channel structure, partner dependencies, or market roles. Avoid dense node labels; use numbered notes.

## 8. driver-tree

Use for decomposing growth, cost, margin, adoption, risk, or operational performance into drivers. Good for explaining why a conclusion holds.

## 9. causal-map

Use for feedback loops, risk transmission, operational dependencies, or strategic cause-effect chains. Mark primary and secondary causal paths.

## 10. market-map

Use for market landscape, segment attractiveness, two-axis opportunity space, or category structure. Can be a 2x2, bubble map, or quadrant.

## 11. competitor-positioning

Use for competitive landscape, capability vs focus, price vs functionality, vertical depth vs platform breadth, or differentiation.

## 12. opportunity-matrix

Use for growth pockets, strategic options, market entry priorities, or product bets. Common axes: impact vs feasibility, attractiveness vs right-to-win.

## 13. risk-heatmap

Use for probability vs impact, severity vs control maturity, or risk prioritization. Define scoring and mitigation owner if possible.

## 14. scenario-grid

Use for upside/base/downside, adoption scenarios, stress cases, market timing, regulatory uncertainty, or investment outcomes.

## 15. option-scorecard

Use for comparing strategic options, acquisition candidates, product bets, market-entry modes, or mitigation choices across criteria.

## 16. decision-brief

Use for board or executive reports. Show decision required, recommendation, evidence, trade-offs, and unresolved questions in one structured block.

## 17. thesis-scorecard

Use for investment memos, M&A screens, or strategic bets. Score market, product, economics, moat, team/execution, and risk evidence.

## 18. unit-economics-bridge

Use for CAC/LTV, gross margin, contribution margin, payback, pricing, cost stack, service-to-software leverage, or profitability path.

## 19. waterfall-bridge

Use for before/after value movement, margin bridge, revenue bridge, cost reductions, value creation, exposure decomposition, or impact attribution.

## 20. funnel-analysis

Use for product, sales, customer journey, conversion, onboarding, usage adoption, or pipeline leakage. Include conversion rates and bottleneck interpretation.

## 21. customer-journey

Use for customer/user workflows, pain points, decision journey, service blueprint, or experience transformation. Pair journey steps with friction and opportunity notes.

## 22. feature-priority-matrix

Use for product roadmap, feature selection, growth initiatives, or capability build. Axes often value vs effort or customer impact vs strategic fit.

## 23. implementation-roadmap

Use for execution plans, workstreams, milestones, transformation sequencing, pilot-to-scale plans, mitigation sequencing, or operating cadence.

## 24. watch-point-tracker

Use for early-warning indicators, investment gates, risk triggers, execution governance, post-decision monitoring, or KPI follow-up.

## Density And Selection Rules

- Normal reports: use 5-8 modules.
- Data-heavy reports: use 8-12 modules and at least five distinct visual types.
- Every major section should usually have at least one exhibit or structured evidence module.
- Do not count raw tables or metric cards as sufficient exhibit diversity.
